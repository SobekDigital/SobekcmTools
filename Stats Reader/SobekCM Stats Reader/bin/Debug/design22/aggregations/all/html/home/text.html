The University of Florida Digital Collections (UFDC) is a library of locally digitized and born digital resources. Excluding commercial library resources available elsewhere, the UFDC holds archival, library and museum materials and other data. The Collections are group topically and refined into narrower collections and subcollections for targetted research. Collection strengths are in Florida and the greater Caribbean basin area, environmental and agricultural sciences, and children's literature. Books, journals, and maps are found among their holdings. Audio and video files, as well as, numeric data may also be found among the Collections.

Collections originate from a variety of sources. The majority originate within the Libraries' Special and Area Studies Collections and from collections in the Florida Museum of Natural Sciences and the Samuel P. Harn Museum of Art. Univerity of Florida colleges and departments, including their faculty and students also contribute to these Collections. The digitized holdings of the Samuel Proctor Oral History Program in the Department of History and the Howard T. Odum Center for Wetlands publications are indicative of this collaboration. Where funding and collaboration make possible, UFDC holdings are enriched by contributions from other educational institutions, government agencies, international programmes, and independent organizations.

Contributions to the UFDC and coordination of collection building and development efforts is the responsibility of the University Libraries' Digital Library Center. Interfaces and search systems are designed in collaboration with the Libraries' web-master and staff from the Libraries' Public Services Division. UFDC systems are maintained and development by the Libraries' Systems Department. Preservation of digital objects is coordinated by the Libraries' Preservation Department in collaboration with the Digital Archive at the Florida Center for Library Automation. Assistance with the description and organization of resources is provided by the cataloging staff or registrars of each of our partners, primarily Libraries' Cataloging & Metadata Department and the archival description program of Special and Area Studies Collections.

Funding for digitization, description and other services is provided by Universty of Florida's George A. Smathers Libraries, other University divisions and departments, and through the effort of each of our partners and their funders and volunteers. Fiscal assistance through granting and related programs comes from the Andrew W. Mellon Foundation; the Institute for Museum and Library Services; the National Endowment for the Humanities; the National Science Foundation; and the State of Florida's Library Services and Technology Act. Additional resources have come through collaboration with organizations such as the Florida Geological Survey; the Florida Humanities Council; the Institute for the Psychological Study of the Arts and the International Conference on Literature and Psychology; and the Rossica Society of Russian Philately. Digitization in the Digital Library Center is funded in part through endowment from the Estate of Governor C. Farris Bryant.
